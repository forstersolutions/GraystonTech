Privacy

Privacy policy for Grayston Technologies.

Last updated June 4, 2026. This page explains what information is collected through this website and how project inquiries are handled.

Contact

Questions about this policy can be sent to jforster@graystontechnologies.com.

Information Collected

Grayston Technologies collects information you choose to submit through the contact form or direct email, including name, email address, company, project type, budget range, timeline, and project details.

The website may also receive ordinary technical data from hosting infrastructure, such as IP address, browser type, device information, request URL, timestamps, and error logs. This site does not intentionally run advertising cookies or sell personal information.

How Information Is Used

  • Respond to project inquiries and schedule follow-up conversations.
  • Scope software, automation, portal, analytics, and security-related work.
  • Operate, debug, secure, and improve the website and contact workflow.
  • Maintain business records related to proposals, contracts, and support.

Sharing

Grayston Technologies does not sell personal information. Information may be processed by service providers that help operate the website, email delivery, hosting, security, or project workflow. Information may also be disclosed if required by law, to protect rights or safety, or with your direction.

Links to MyPokerMaps, FeltOS, CUOPeration, QrystalDrop, VerityForge-related resources, and other external sites are governed by those sites' own privacy practices.

Retention And Security

Inquiry data is kept as long as needed for business follow-up, project records, legal obligations, security, and operational continuity. Reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards are used, but no internet service can guarantee absolute security.

Your Choices

You may request access, correction, or deletion of contact inquiry information by emailing jforster@graystontechnologies.com. Requests may be limited where records are needed for security, legal, contractual, or legitimate business reasons.

Do not send highly sensitive secrets, credentials, regulated data, or confidential third-party information through the contact form unless a separate agreement and secure intake process are in place.
